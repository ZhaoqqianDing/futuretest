Public Transport

 

Introduction

Melbourne's public transport system includes rail, tram and bus services. Its tram network is the largest in the world. Nearly 300 bus lines and 16 rail lines serve Greater Melbourne. Melbourne's metropolitan area has a well-developed public transport network, with lanes and walking paths criss-crossed, making travel convenient and economical.

Metro

Melbourne has an urban heavy rail network of 16 lines, operated by Melbourne Metro Trains and served by a fleet of 326 EMU trains. The urban network is considered a commuter-heavy rail hybrid rather than a subway system with high-frequency and high-capacity services.

Tram

Melbourne has the world's largest tram network, comprising nearly 500 trams and 1,763 tram stops on 250 kilometres (160 miles) of double track, 24 lines. It is run by Keolis Downer. Two partial light rail routes are also part of the network. Most of the rest of the track is mixed with general traffic, which also makes it one of the slowest tram networks in the world. Trams run mainly in suburban areas, usually providing short to medium distances.

Bus

Buses in Melbourne, Australia are a major form of public transport in Melbourne with an extensive bus network. There are 346 routes with different frequencies of service operated by private bus companies franchised by the state government.

Myki Card - your transport ticket in Melbourne

myki is a Melbourne train, tram and bus ticket. It is a reusable smart card that stores the value used to pay for public transport and can be used repeatedly across all services.

Myki stores two types of value on one card - Myki pass (travel days) and/or Myki money (dollar amount).
Myki pass

myki pass stores the number of travel days in a specified area and is valid for a certain period of time. You specify the travel area and the exact duration of the pass - 7 consecutive days or 28 to 365 consecutive days.

Myki money

myki money is the dollar value stored on myki to pay for your ride when you travel.

With myki money, you don't have to consider time or region, as the correct fare for your itinerary is calculated both when and after you depart. Fares are calculated based on where and when you travel and the correct fare is automatically deducted from your myki currency balance.

myki money will initially be charged for 2 hours. If further travel is made beyond 2 hours, it will be upgraded and capped at the daily fare.

You can purchase myki from:

● Nearly 800 retailers with the myki logo, including all 7-Eleven stores

● Ticket windows at Premium train stations

● myki machines at all Metropolitan train stations and some accessible tram and bus interchanges (full price myki only)

● Online or call 1800 800 007 (for Australian addresses only).

You will need to top up myki before travelling to pay for the ride.Make sure you top up with enough value to cover your day's travel expenses.
