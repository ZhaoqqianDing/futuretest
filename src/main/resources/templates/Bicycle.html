Cycling

 

Introduction

The City of Melbourne’s cycling network has over 135 km of on- and off-road routes. Victoria government are investing in new and upgraded bike paths, lanes and routes to improve connections and safety for all cyclists.

Cycling is a great way to get around the city as well as keep fit. The City of Melbourne’s cycling network has over 135 km of on and off-road routes. So get out, get active and move your own way.

Cycling/parking range

Parking Range

Various bike parking facilities are available in the City of Melbourne, with more than 2700 bicycle hoops on streets across the municipality.

Cycling Range

Major road cycle routes include Royal Parade, Canning Street, Rathdowne Street, Swanston Street, Albert Street, La Trobe Street, Exhibition Street and St Kilda Road.

Cross-country trails include the Yarra River Trail, Moonee Ponds Creek Trail, Maribyrnong River Trail, Dynon Road, Footscray Road and Harbour Esplanade.

The Capital City Trail (PDF 354 KB) is a shared path for cyclists and pedestrians around the centre of Melbourne and some of Melbourne's inner eastern and northern suburbs. It is 29 km long and consists mainly of other trails such as Merri Creek Trail, Main Yarra Trail, Moonee Ponds Creek Trail and Inner Circle Rail Trail.

Change to the law

There are changes to rules about riding on footpaths in Victoria. You can ride on a footpath if you:

✔ are a child under 13

✔ are 13 or over and riding with a child who is under 13

✔ are 18 or over and riding with a young child in a child seat attached to your bicycle or you have a child pedalling on a bike attachment connected to your bicycle

✔ have a medical certificate that says you have a disability that makes it difficult to ride on the road. You must follow the conditions on your medical certificate and carry a copy with you to show to police or an authorised person if asked

✔ are 13 or over and riding with someone who has a medical certificate that says they have a disability that makes it difficult to ride on the road.

Driving Precautions

Locking your bike

Always lock your bike with a good D-lock or armoured cable. Using a cable and D-lock together is even more secure.

Secure your bike in visible areas with lots of people around, and avoid locking your bike to sucker poles as they can be easily lifted out of the ground. Remember to remove your lights and check your lock before leaving.

The police can assist in the recovery of stolen bikes. If your bike is stolen, the theft should be reported to the police as soon as possible.

The City of Melbourne TravelSmart Map includes some handy tips about security for bikes.

Report an issue

You can report issues with bike parking, including:

● bicycle hoops in need of cleaning, maintenance or repairs

● abandoned bicycles for removal.
